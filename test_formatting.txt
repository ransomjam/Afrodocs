INTERNSHIP PROGRAM OVERVIEW

1. Types of Internships:
   1. Paid Internships: Paid internships involve the intern receiving financial compensation for their work. Larger corporations and government agencies typically offer paid internships to their interns. This helps to attract more qualified interns and shows that the organization values the work of the interns.
   
   2. Unpaid Internships: Unpaid internships do not provide financial compensation to the intern for their work. Instead, interns may receive academic credit or gain valuable work experience and skills that will help them in their future career.
   
   3. Virtual Internships: Virtual internships, also known as remote internships, allow interns to work remotely from their own location rather than having to physically go to an office or workplace.

2. Aims and Objectives:
   1. Personal Development: One of the primary aims of an internship is to help individuals gain a better understanding of their own interests, strengths, and areas for improvement. Through hands-on experience in a real work environment, interns are able to assess their personal preferences, work habits, and career goals.
   
   2. Educational Development: Internships are designed to complement academic learning with practical experience, bridging the gap between theory and practice. The objectives of educational development through internships include applying classroom knowledge to real-world scenarios, mastering industry-specific skills, and gaining insight into the latest trends and advancements in the field.
   
   3. Professional Development: Internships play a crucial role in preparing individuals for the demands of the workforce and equipping them with the necessary skills to succeed in their chosen career paths.
